Deliverables
A functional tone of voice and reading level tool
Description
Our company banned AI writing tools. These days it’s nearly impossible to find a writing tool that doesn’t use AI, and we needed something to help quickly validate writing within our product. I developed and deployed a tool that checks for tone of voice, grade reading level, and grammar/spelling issues in order to help Designers & PMs adhere to UX writing standards.

Problem

No AI writing tools allowed.

Paycom is a $2B/yr provider of HR & HCM software. The I.T. risk department stopped use of all AI tools to prevent employees from leaking sensitive data. As such, the tool we were used to check if our in-system writing matched up to our guidelines (Hemingway Editor AI) became banned.

×

Detrimental Impact

Reduced operating efficiency.

As a result of these tools being banned, PMs & designers now had to spend more time every week getting together to ask others if writing was up to standard. In a fast paced work environment, every minute is precious. Something had to change.

×

Why it's important

Consistency & comprehension.

UX writing standards are important because the way we speak to users must be consistent across our system. It must avoid placing blame on anyone for any errors. It must be brief, understandable by all, and actionable. We strive for a 5th grade reading level on all non-admin facing tools. It’s a lot of boxes to tick to ensure the user doesn’t feel lost or unsure, so validation should be super simple.

×

Ideal world

Immediate feedback.

With all other relevant tools containing undesired AI features – and thus being blocked – I wanted to empower designers & PMs to quickly and independently know if they’re adhering to writing standards.

×

Requirements

Free, no AI integration, no storing data.

The tool had to be free to use and to host, not rely on AI, and not store any data anywhere. With that in mind, I created a web tool that locally calculated reading level using the Flesch–Kincaid formula. I called on a free, trusted, open source API to check for tone of voice & spelling/grammar issues, and hosted it all on Github Pages.

×

Solution

One click automation.

No more waiting on people to answer Teams messages about what tone of voice someone thinks a sentence is in. Over the course of a week, I built Hemingnay (get it? because it’s not hemingway) and now Designers & PMs have immediate feedback. I have since pushed several maintenance updates based on user feedback.

×

Business Impact

Estimated $4,350 yearly saved.

The average PM & designer spends approximately 30 minutes per week asking & answering others about writing standards. Multiply that by 200 people making an estimated average of $90,000 per year, and we’re saving $4,350 in operating costs with this free, simple tool. That’s enough to buy 2 of my car!

×

Future goals

Personalized suggestions.

It would be neat if my tool could emulate all of the (lesser used) functions of the tool we used previously. While not as important as the high level reading level, tone of voice, and spellcheck tools. I would love it if the tool could see your sentence, determine that it’s too long or hard to understand, and then spit out a few better suggestions.

×

Lessons learned

Keep it stupid simple.

I originally created a less user-friendly and much uglier v1. I gave users the option to switch APIs, and the option to select what tone of voice to check for. People weren’t sure what options did, so in my redesign, I opted to just keep it at one button and give immediate high level information.

×

Final reflection

Owning something is exciting.

It felt extremely cool to be able to be the designer, developer, and strategist behind a product people actually rely on to get their work done. The biggest thing about being behind a product (big, or in my case, minuscule) is remembering that it exists to serve the user, not your idealistic vision. Detaching from the ego of being the mastermind behind a project is crucial to success. This project will continue to serve users and receive updates informed by people’s needs and usage patterns.

Thank you for reading!
