Purpose

What is this for?

The Guided Product Tour, a.k.a. In-system-guidance (ISG) was designed to provide real-time, contextual learning for users within Paycom’s products. Its goal is to:

- Reduce the number of customer service inquiries by providing immediate in-product guidance.
- Enhance user experience by delivering structured, non-intrusive assistance.
- Ensure consistent and effective product adoption through structured learning flows.

×

Different problems, different solutions.

Tiered approach.

ISG is categorized into three tiers based on the complexity and depth of the guidance provided:

Tier 1: One-Time Notifications
- Used for crucial updates and system-wide notifications.
- Does not include a toggle - meant for one-time viewing.

Tier 2: Guided Walkthroughs
- Provides step-by-step interactive walkthroughs for product features.
- Uses a basic toggle to allow users to revisit the guidance when needed.

Tier 3: Advanced Learning Flows
- Offers multiple learning paths within a single product.
- Utilizes a dropdown toggle to allow users to select the appropriate flow.
- Some flows may be disabled until certain conditions are met.

×

Preparing for consistency & success

Establishing content guidelines.

Content guidelines were set in place - things ranging from use of external vs internal links, the thought behind opening links in a new tab, what % opacity to use for an overlay, how to provide context, and more.

×

Nitty gritty

Verbiage.

Some guidelines were set in place for how we speak to the user. Things like conciseness, consistent formatting of text/titles/descriptions, tone of voice, and being empathetic towards the user as to not make them feel ashamed or discouraged.

×

Scale

Accessibility considerations.

Ensuring ISG is accessible was and is a key priority. We must meet WCAG (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines) 2.2 AA standards.

×

Documentation

Ensuring easy updates.

A 65-page documentation was created covering every aspect of ISG design, from research to testing. This documentation proved extremely useful as it was constantly being referenced when getting buy-in from development leadership as to why it may be an important thing to build, and how exactly to build it.

The good ending

Reflection.

Throughout this project, I conducted research, designed and iterated on prototypes, and prepared an MVP for handoff from 0-1. Despite initial concerns about meeting deadlines, expectations were exceeded to the point where the project’s scope could grow even larger. Seeing it as a live feature makes me very happy!
